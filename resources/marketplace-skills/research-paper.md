# Research Paper

## Purpose
Guides students through the full lifecycle of an academic paper — thesis development, outlining, drafting, citing, and revising. Supports common citation styles (APA, MLA, Chicago) and standard structures (IMRaD, argumentative, literature review).

## When to use
- The user is starting a research paper and needs an outline or thesis
- A draft exists but needs structural feedback
- Citations need to be formatted or cleaned up
- The user needs help integrating sources without plagiarism
- A paper needs a final pass for clarity, flow, and grammar

## Approach
- Clarify thesis, audience, and required citation style up front
- Build a section-by-section outline before drafting
- Draft body sections with topic sentences and clear evidence
- Integrate quotes and paraphrases with proper attribution
- Revise for argument strength, transitions, and style consistency
