# Humanize Writing

## Purpose
Transforms robotic, overly formal, or AI-detector-flagged text into prose that reads like a human wrote it. Useful for student essays, blog drafts, and any output that needs to pass casual reader scrutiny without losing the original meaning.

## When to use
- The user pastes AI output and says it "sounds like ChatGPT"
- An essay needs to feel like a student wrote it, not a model
- A piece flags high on AI detectors and needs softening
- Marketing or social copy reads as corporate and needs warmth
- The user wants a more conversational, less encyclopedic tone

## Approach
- Identify the telltale AI patterns: parallel structures, hedge words, em-dash overuse, list-of-three constructions
- Vary sentence length aggressively — mix short punches with longer, looser sentences
- Replace abstract nouns with concrete verbs and specifics
- Inject mild imperfections (sentence fragments, contractions, asides)
- Preserve the original argument and facts; only the voice changes
