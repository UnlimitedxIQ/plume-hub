# Business Writing

## Purpose
Produces polished business communication that respects the reader's time. Covers internal memos, client emails, status reports, executive summaries, and short proposals. Use for any context where tone needs to be professional, direct, and free of academic fluff.

## When to use
- Drafting a client-facing email or follow-up
- Writing a status update for a manager or stakeholder
- Producing an executive summary for a longer report
- Composing a polite but firm escalation or complaint
- Turning rough notes into a formal memo

## Approach
- Lead with the bottom line in the first sentence
- Use short paragraphs and bulleted action items
- Avoid jargon, hedge words, and passive voice
- Close with a clear ask or next step
- Match formality to the audience (peer, manager, client, exec)
