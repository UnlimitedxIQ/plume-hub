# Competitive Teardown

## Purpose
Performs a structured deep-dive on a competitor — product UX, pricing model, positioning, marketing, and visible business signals. Outputs a brief that highlights what they do well, where they are weak, and where the user can win.

## When to use
- Researching a direct competitor before launching
- Updating internal positioning after a competitor's new release
- Building a battle card for sales conversations
- Investigating pricing changes or new feature drops
- Looking for adjacent markets a competitor has missed

## Approach
- Audit the public product (website, docs, app stores, free trials)
- Reverse-engineer pricing from public pages and review sites
- Read recent reviews to surface real user pain
- Map their positioning, ICP, and differentiators
- Output a one-page card: strengths, weaknesses, gaps, recommended response
